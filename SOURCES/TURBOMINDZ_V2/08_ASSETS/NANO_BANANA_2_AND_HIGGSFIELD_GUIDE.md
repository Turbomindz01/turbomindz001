## PART 1: NANO BANANA 2 — Image Generation

### What Is It?
Nano Banana 2 is Google DeepMind's image generation model. It generates images in 1-5 seconds at up to 4K resolution. Available free on Google AI Studio, fal.ai, and other platforms.

### Key Differences from Midjourney
| Aspect | Midjourney | Nano Banana 2 |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| Prompt style | Tag lists + flags | Descriptive prose sentences |
| Parameters | --ar 4:5 --v 7 --s 350 --q 2 | Set in UI (no flags in prompt) |
| Speed | 18-24 seconds | 1-5 seconds |
| Text in images | Unreliable | 94% accuracy |
| Access | Discord only | Web UI, API, multiple platforms |

### How to Access (Free)
1. **Google AI Studio** — ai.google.dev/aistudio (free, requires Google account)
2. **fal.ai** — fal.ai/models/fal-ai/nano-banana-2 (free tier available)
3. **Freepik** — freepik.com (limited free generations)

### Prompt Format Rules

**DO:**
- Write 1-3 descriptive sentences
- Use photographic/cinematic language ("warm volumetric lighting," "low angle composition")
- Describe subject, action, setting, style, mood, lighting in that order
- Be specific about colors (use hex values or descriptive names)
- Prioritize important details early in the prompt (first details have most influence)

**DON'T:**
- Use Midjourney flags (--ar, --v, --s, --q) — these go in the UI settings
- Use "tag soup" (comma-separated keywords)
- Use quality boosters ("masterpiece," "trending on ArtStation," "8K")
- Use weighted syntax or negative weights

### Turbomindz Style Anchor

Include this phrase (or variation) in EVERY prompt to maintain visual consistency:

> "Plasticine stop-motion animation style, Pixar meets Aardman studios, warm rich clay textures, museum-quality detail"

### Color Direction

Always include the dominant colors for the scene:
- **Teal/Gold (default):** "deep teal #004D5C and gold #C9A227 color palette"
- **By theme:** Reference the specific theme color from the brand system

### Camera/Composition Direction

Use cinematic language:
- "Low angle dramatic composition" (makes subjects feel heroic)
- "Bird's eye view" (for establishing shots)
- "Close-up intimate framing" (for character moments)
- "Wide establishing shot" (for universe introductions)
- "Over-the-shoulder perspective" (for dialogue scenes)

### Lighting Direction

Always specify:
- "Warm volumetric golden light rays" (default Turbomindz mood)
- "Dramatic chiaroscuro" (for Renaissance universes)
- "Soft diffused lighting" (for Eastern/meditative universes)
- "Bioluminescent glow" (for botanical universes)
- "Neon and candlelight contrast" (for modern/mixed universes)

### Settings in the UI

For every generation, set these in the platform UI (NOT in the prompt):

| Setting | Value | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Aspect Ratio | 4:5 | Standard for NFT scenes |
| Resolution | 2K (default) or 4K (key scenes) | 4K for Episode 1, milestones, finales |
| Temperature | Medium | Balance of creativity and consistency |

Use 16:9 for website hero images and episode thumbnails.
Use 1:1 for character portraits and social media avatars.

### 10 Example Prompts (Turbomindz Style)

**1. Universe Establishing Shot:**
> A vast Renaissance alchemical cathedral interior seen from the entrance, with golden alchemical symbols glowing on stone walls, bubbling glass flasks on ancient wooden tables, warm candlelight reflecting off brass astrolabes and copper retorts. Deep blue and gold color palette with mysterious floor-level fog. A sense of ancient wisdom preserved in amber light. Plasticine stop-motion animation style, Pixar meets Aardman studios, dramatic chiaroscuro lighting, warm rich clay textures, museum-quality detail.

**2. Character Arrival:**
> A small claymation astronaut with orange boots and a fishbowl helmet stepping through a glowing golden doorway into a new universe. The doorway frames them from behind, their silhouette backlit by warm golden light. The new world ahead is visible but slightly blurred, suggesting discovery. Curious body language, one hand reaching forward. Deep teal and gold color palette. Plasticine stop-motion style with volumetric golden light rays.

**3. Two Characters in Dialogue:**
> A claymation astronaut with orange boots sitting across from a claymation alien guide with three floating golden orbs and an infinity pendant, both on ancient stone steps in a geometric palace. They are in animated conversation, gestures expressive. Islamic geometric star patterns tessellate on the walls behind them in emerald and gold tiles. Warm candlelight from above creates intimate shadows. Plasticine stop-motion style, warm and contemplative mood.

**4. The Fade Appearing:**
> A claymation scene where color is draining from the edges of a bioluminescent garden. The left side of the frame is vibrant — glowing mushrooms, luminescent ferns, emerald and gold. The right side fades to grey monochrome, as if the color is being erased by an invisible hand. A small astronaut with orange boots stands at the boundary between color and grey, looking alarmed. The mood shifts from wonder to concern. Plasticine stop-motion style with split lighting — warm golden on the left, cold grey on the right.

**5. The Forge Moment (Act of Creation):**
> A claymation astronaut kneeling on the floor of a Renaissance cathedral, assembling broken glass pieces into a mosaic pattern. Golden light emanates from the completed sections of the mosaic. The astronaut's hands are clay-textured and imperfect. Behind them, the cathedral pulses with returning color. The mood is desperate hope becoming confident creation. Close-up framing on the hands and mosaic. Plasticine stop-motion style, warm golden lighting from the mosaic itself.

**6. Universe Detail (Art Tradition Focus):**
> A close-up of a wall covered in intricate Japanese sumi-e ink wash, where black ink mountains dissolve into white mist and a giant calligraphy brush made of starlight paints new constellations into existence. The ink flows like liquid glass. Zen garden elements float among the stars — raked sand patterns, carefully placed stones. Orange and teal accent colors against the black and white ink. Plasticine textures visible in the clay-like brush and stones.

**7. Character Emotional Beat:**
> A claymation alien guide with three floating golden orbs sitting alone in a neon void. Two of her orbs glow warmly, but the third is dim and flickering. Her posture suggests deep sadness — shoulders curved inward, infinity pendant hanging still. The void around her is absolute black with distant neon calligraphy characters in orange and purple. The mood is intimate grief. Single source of warm golden light from the two active orbs.

**8. Community/Village Scene:**
> Four claymation characters gathered around a simple wooden table in a warm village. String lights above. A notebook lies open. A pencil rests beside it. Through the window, 39 glowing islands are visible against a deep teal night sky. The characters' body language suggests comfortable silence — they've been together a long time. Warm golden hour lighting, cozy atmosphere. Plasticine stop-motion style.

**9. The Chorus (Golden Threads):**
> Golden light threads stretching between floating islands in a vast deep teal cosmos. Each thread vibrates with a warm glow, connecting universes of different art styles — one Renaissance, one cybernetic, one made of origami paper. The threads form a web that resembles both a neural network and a musical score. Small sparks of light travel along the threads like messages. Wide establishing shot showing the full network. Plasticine stop-motion style with ethereal golden volumetric lighting.

**10. Season Finale Dramatic Shot:**
> A claymation astronaut standing at the very edge of existence — one foot on colorful ground, the other over a shimmering grey void that isn't empty but iridescent, full of unnamed colors. Behind the astronaut, three companions watch. The grey void reflects light in ways that suggest transformation rather than destruction. The astronaut's posture shifts from fear to curiosity. Low angle dramatic composition. Plasticine stop-motion style with split lighting — warm gold behind, cool iridescence ahead.

## PART 2: HIGGSFIELD.AI — Film Production

### What Is It?
Higgsfield.ai is an AI video generation platform that converts still images into animated video clips. It aggregates multiple AI models (Kling 3.0, Veo 3.1, WAN 2.5, Sora 2) in one workspace.

### Why Higgsfield for Turbomindz
- **Image-to-Video:** Upload your Nano Banana 2 images and animate them
- **Soul ID:** Train character consistency so Cosmo always looks like Cosmo
- **Multi-Shot:** Generate multiple camera angles from one image
- **Free tier:** Multiple daily generations, up to 20 seconds at 1080p

### Pricing Consideration
- **Free tier:** Sufficient for initial production (multiple daily generations)
- **Creator plan (~$19-29/mo):** If you need higher volume — still under $50/mo budget
- Start free. Upgrade only if volume demands it.

### Initial Setup (One-Time)

**Step 1: Create Character References**

For EACH of the 4 characters, generate 10+ reference images in Nano Banana 2 showing different angles and expressions:

- Front view, neutral expression
- 3/4 view, both sides
- Full body, standing
- Full body, sitting
- Close-up of face/helmet
- Action pose (reaching, creating)
- Emotional states (curious, sad, determined, joyful)

Save these to your `turbomindz-brand-assets` folder.

**Step 2: Train Soul ID**

1. Go to Higgsfield.ai → Soul ID
2. Upload the 10+ reference images for Cosmo
3. Name the Soul ID: "Cosmo_Turbomindz"
4. Let it train (takes a few minutes)
5. Repeat for Nova, Stella, Luna
6. Repeat for key locations (U1 cathedral, generic village, etc.)

This is the MOST IMPORTANT step. Good Soul IDs = consistent characters across 278 days of production.

**Step 3: Set Up Location Anchors**

For each universe, generate one "hero frame" — the definitive establishing shot. Lock this as a Reference Anchor in Higgsfield. When generating video for that universe's episode, always reference this anchor.

### Daily Video Production Workflow

For each of the 4 daily NFT images:

**1. Upload the Image**
- Go to Higgsfield.ai
- Select "Image to Video"
- Upload your Nano Banana 2 image

**2. Write the Video Prompt**
Keep it simple. Describe the MOTION, not the scene (the image already provides the scene):

Good prompts:
- "Slow push-in. Candle flames flicker. Fog drifts at floor level. The astronaut shifts weight slightly."
- "Gentle pan left to right across the geometric tiles. Light refracts through the patterns. Characters turn heads slowly."
- "Camera orbits the characters at table height. Golden thread between them shimmers. Notebook pages flutter."

Bad prompts:
- "A Renaissance cathedral with golden symbols..." (the image already shows this)

**3. Select Model**

| Situation | Model | Why |
|-----------|-------|-----|
| Default / most scenes | WAN 2.5 | Best continuity, good quality |
| Key scenes (Forge moments) | Veo 3.1 | Highest visual quality |
| Character dialogue | Kling 3.0 | Best for subtle expressions |
| Fast preview | Any | Render at 480p first |

**4. Settings**
- Duration: 10-15 seconds (we'll stitch later)
- Resolution: 480p for preview, 1080p for final
- Aspect ratio: Match the NFT image (4:5 for portrait, 16:9 for establishing)

**5. Reference Setup**
- Enable Soul ID for any character visible
- Set Reference Anchor to the episode's establishing shot
- If multi-character scene: only show one character's face per clip

**6. Generate and Review**
- Generation takes 2-5 minutes
- Watch for: character drift, weird motion, texture loss
- If good → save. If not → adjust prompt and regenerate.
- Typical: 1-2 attempts per clip

### Multi-Shot Feature (For Key Scenes)

For important scenes (Forge moments, character introductions), use Multi-Shot to get multiple camera angles from one image:

1. Upload the reference image
2. Write 3-4 different camera angle prompts:
   - "Wide establishing shot, static camera"
   - "Close-up on character's hands creating"
   - "Over-the-shoulder looking at the creation"
   - "Low angle looking up at the universe responding"
3. Set clip length to 10 seconds each
4. Render at 480p first, approve, then upscale to 1080p

**Best Practices for Multi-Shot:**
- Only show a character's face in ONE of the shots (prevents drift)
- Keep to one character per shot when possible
- Set same duration for all shots in a batch

### Weekly Episode Assembly (DaVinci Resolve)

Every 7th day, assemble the week's 28 video clips into one episode:

**1. Import all 28 clips** into DaVinci Resolve (free version)

**2. Arrange by shot list:**
- Shots 1-3: THE ARRIVAL (establishing, enter, react)
- Shots 4-7: THE WONDER (explore, discover, detail, question)
- Shots 8-10: THE QUESTION (quote, dialogue, contemplate)
- Shots 11-14: THE DEEPENING (character, interaction, discovery, emotional peak)
- Shots 15-17: THE FADE APPEARS (first sign, manifestation, realization)
- Shots 18-21: THE STRUGGLE (attempt, certainty, conflict, darkest moment)
- Shots 22-25: THE FORGE (inspiration, begin, creation, response)
- Shots 26-28: THE WITNESS (resolution, reflection, transition)

**3. Add transitions:**
- Cross-dissolve between segments (0.5 second)
- Hard cuts within segments (more dynamic)
- Fade to black between seasons

**4. Color grading:**
- Ensure consistent teal-and-gold palette
- Desaturate Fade scenes progressively
- Warm the Forge moments (golden highlights)

**5. Audio:**
- Higgsfield generates native audio with each clip
- Layer with royalty-free ambient music (freemusicarchive.org)
- Keep dialogue-free (the series is visual storytelling)
- Add subtle Chorus hum in transitions

**6. Export:**
- 1080p, H.264, 24fps
- Duration target: 2-5 minutes
- Filename: `TMDZ_S01E01_The_First_Question.mp4`

**7. Upload to YouTube:**
- Title: "Turbomindz | Episode 1: The First Question | The Odyssey of Light"
- Description: Episode synopsis + links to NFTs on OpenSea
- Tags: turbomindz, nft art, philosophical animation, claymation, web3
- Thumbnail: Best NFT image from the episode (16:9 crop)
